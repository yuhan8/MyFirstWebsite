A big community for people who own pets

Geo-tagged features, helping you taking care of your pets when you need it.

Pet care community
in your neighborhood.

LIFE OF PET is an iOS geo-tagged application that helps people who own pets when they need them, which also provides users pet care store information and other people who are also using this app in the neighborhood.

User Research

We spent 3 weeks on user research in total. For this project, we included two sessions of pre-design user research, which are one survey contains 14 questions and interview sessions. We received 46 answers for the survey, and we conducted three 30 minutes long intervews with three different participants.

Data analysis of the result of our user research was not too complicated. Among all the data we collected, we had different forms of bar charts, pie charts etc. to visualize the result. I am showing four pie charts here to indicate some of the important findings.

Based on the result of data analysis, we noticed that among the 46 participants who submitted their survey answers, there are more than half, 58.7%, of the participants ranked information about Pet Care Service in their local area as the most needed feature. With this in mind, we decided to merge this feature as one of the most important one of our later design.

Besides sending out surveys, we also conducted three 30 minutes sessions of interview with 3 participants. During these interviews, we collected data about all different types of information that are involved with daily life of taking care of their pets.

Design Decisions

We used the result of user research and data analysis to start our brain storming process. Before jump into the design process, we first defined the problems that appear to be crucial to the product. Design Process

We conducted our design process based on this info graphic below. We iterated on the sessions "usability test" to "redesign" many times, until we reached at the stage that we are finally satiesfied with the final result.

Brainstorm

After fully analyzing our data, we started our brainstorm process, mainly focusing on the three different problems and solutions. We spent 5 minutes on writing down all the ideas we have, one idea at one post-it.
After this five minutes of rapid brainstorm, we then sticked all the post-its on a white board and started picking out promising ideas and started another session of 5 minutes brainstorm on these ideas. We iterated this process for 5 times, which led to a promising and structured information architecture that we will going to utilize on the product.
